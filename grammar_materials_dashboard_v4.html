📖 Model Examples READ-ONLY

These examples demonstrate high-quality responses based on the Passive Voice (SMA XI, B1, Environment). Use them as a benchmark for your own work.

Step 1 Model: Structure & Context

Grammar: Passive voice | Grade: SMA Grade XI | Level: B1

Theme: Environment | Class Size: 40 students

Step 2 Model: Larsen-Freeman Analysis

DimensionAnalysis
FORMSubject + to be + past participle.
Example: 'Forests in Kalimantan are destroyed by illegal logging.'
MEANINGThe action is emphasized over the agent; the agent is unknown, unimportant, or deliberately omitted.
USEAcademic writing, news reports, scientific texts, formal announcements.
CHALLENGEBahasa Indonesia uses the di- prefix with no obligatory 'to be', so students tend to write 'Trees cut down' instead of 'Trees are cut down'.

Step 3 Model: PPP Design

🟦 Presentation:

Input: 'Every year, millions of trees are cut down in Sumatra. Rare animals are forced out of their homes. New laws have been introduced by the government to stop illegal logging.'

Rule: When the action matters more than the doer, use Subject + is/are + past participle (+ by + agent).

CCQ: In "New laws have been introduced", who introduced the laws? Why don't we know?

🟨 Practice (Gap-fill):

1. Millions of trees are cut down in Sumatra. | 2. National parks are protected. | 3. Waste is dumped. | 4. Solutions have been introduced. | 5. Orangutans are forced out.

🟩 Production:

Task: In pairs, describe an environmental problem using passive voice. (Current problem, cause, action).

Frame: '[Place] is/are ___ by ___. One solution that has been ___ is ___.'

Note: Display frame. Pairs share. Teacher notes 3-5 common errors for post-task feedback.

Step 4 Model: Activities (Controlled, Guided, Communicative)

Activity 1: Controlled (Error Correction)

PRE: Vote on board: 'The report writes by manager' vs 'is written'. Why?

WHILST: Correct: 1. bridge build → was built. 2. fabric are dye → is dyed. 3. school is build → is being built. 4. animals force → are forced. 5. pempek made → is made.

POST: Peer check.

Activity 2: Guided (Sentence Writing)

PRE: Photos of Batik, Pempek, Songket. Predict process.

WHILST: Product → Process → Location: 1. Batik → dye by hand → Yogyakarta ('Batik cloth is dyed...'). 2. Pempek → make → Palembang. 3. Songket → weave → S. Sumatra. 4. Tempe → ferment → Java. 5. Wayang → carve → Java.

POST: Partner checks form.

Activity 3: Communicative (Information Gap)

PRE: Review frame. Brainstorm 5 products.

WHILST: Student A/B gap. A asks 'How is songket made?' B answers. Exchange all 6 items, write 2 summary sentences.

POST: Compare answers. Teacher board correction. Write 1 original sentence without frame.

Step 5 Model: Reflection

Q1 (LO1): Analysis showed passive isn't just structural; learners miss why action, not doer, is foregrounded in formal texts.

Q2 (LO2): Difficulty is the missing auxiliary. Because Indonesian lacks 'be' in passives, we built extra noticing/correction practice.

Q3 (LO3): PPP moves from noticing in context → controlled practice → pair task where students use structure for real messages.

Q4 (LO4/5): Pre-activity builds readiness, whilst-activity provides scaffolding, post-activity consolidates accuracy without interrupting communication flow.
