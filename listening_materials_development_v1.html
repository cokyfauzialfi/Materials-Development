Listening Materials Development Project

Design a complete listening material for Indonesian school learners

📖 Project Overview

This take-home group project asks you to design a complete listening material for Indonesian school learners. Your product must connect a target listening sub-skill, learner challenge, school level, classroom context, and three-stage activity sequence.

90 Minutes
4-5 Students
SD / SMP / SMA
A1-A2 Beginner / B1 Intermediate

Step 1: Choose Your Listening Text and Context

Select a short listening text suitable for your target learners (15 minutes)

Important: Select a short listening text that supports both gist and selected-detail tasks. It may be teacher-provided, adapted, teacher-created, or semi-authentic.

Step 2: Design Your Pre-Activity

Prepare students to listen successfully (10-15 minutes)

Important: Prepare students without giving away all content. Introduce 3-4 essential words/clues and set a clear listening purpose.

Step 3: Design Your While-Activity

Gist + Detail Listening Tasks (25 minutes)

Important: Design two tasks using the same listening text. Activity A focuses on gist. Activity B focuses on 3-4 selected details.

🔍 Activity A: Gist Listening

📝 Activity B: Selected Detail Listening

Step 4: Design Your Post-Activity and Revision

Extend learning beyond answer checking (15 minutes)

Important: Design one short follow-up task that asks students to reuse ideas or useful language from the listening. Add one revision note based on likely classroom response.

Step-by-Step Examples

💡 Example: Step 1 (SMP Grade VIII)

School level/grade: SMP Grade VIII

Proficiency level: A2-B1

Topic: Indonesian traditional foods

Text type: Short interview

Title/short description of audio: A student talks about pempek.

Target listening sub-skill: Gist and selected-detail comprehension

Main listening challenge: Students may already know the food, but they may miss descriptive details such as ingredients, place of origin, and reasons for recommendation because of natural speech rate and connected speech.

Classroom constraint/ support needed: The class is large and uses one speaker only. Students need pair checking before whole-class feedback so that more students can compare and confirm their answers.

Material decision: Semi-authentic/ teacher-created

Reason for decision: The topic is familiar and culturally relevant for Indonesian students, while the short interview format provides realistic spoken input that can support both gist listening and selected-detail listening.

💡 Example: Step 2

Pre-listening focus: Preparing students to understand key descriptive details in a short interview about pempek.

Key words/ clues: ingredients, savory, famous for, spicy

Learner challenge supported: Students may miss descriptive details because some key words are unfamiliar or difficult to recognize in natural speech.

Presentation method: Show one photo of pempek and one photo of cuko sauce. Briefly introduce the four key words using the pictures and simple examples.

Check question: If a food is well known in many places, what phrase can we use?

Answer: Famous for

Student-facing instructions: Look at the picture of pempek. What do you already know about this food? Match the four words with their meanings: ingredients, savory, famous for, and spicy. Then predict two details the speaker may mention.

Worked model: Pempek is famous for its cuko sauce. The speaker may mention its ingredients and its taste.

Brief rationale: This activity prepares students with only the most important words and clues. It helps them predict the content without giving away all the information from the listening text.

💡 Example: Step 3

Activity A (Gist): Activity type: Gist listening. Purpose: Identify main topic and general reason. Items: 1. What food? (b. Pempek) 2. Why like it? (b. Savory & reminds of family meals). Rationale: Reduces pressure, catches main idea first.

Activity B (Detail): Activity type: Selected-detail listening. Purpose: Identify specific info after understanding topic. Items: 1. Food name (Pempek) 2. Origin (Palembang) 3. Ingredients (Fish, tapioca) 4. Reason (Tasty & famous). Large-class note: Pair check before whole-class feedback.

💡 Example: Step 4

Post-activity type: Short speaking response

Post-listening purpose: Students use ideas and expressions from the listening text to describe a local food from their own area.

Student-facing instructions: Work in pairs. Choose one local food from your town. Describe it in two or three sentences. Use at least one expression from the listening, such as famous for, savory, spicy, or made from.

Worked model: Burgo is famous in Palembang. It is soft and savory, and people often eat it for breakfast.

Revision after classroom use: If students understand the main idea but miss important details, shorten the detail task or replay only the relevant section of the audio in the next use of the material.

Brief rationale: Extends listening into meaningful speaking practice while connecting to local context.
