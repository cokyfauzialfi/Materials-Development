Indonesian ELT Classroom

"Language and culture are fundamentally integrated." — Liddicoat (032)
Activity 1

The Cultural Mediator

Description

Students navigate a cultural mismatch between Indonesian and Australian norms. This activity focuses on the Mediator role, encouraging linguistic and intercultural bridge-building (Wang Ge, 2004).

  • Role: Host (Indo)
  • Role: Guest (AUS)
  • Role: Mediator
Activity 2

The Cultural Mirror

Reflection Mechanism

Using the ID Model (Houghton, 2013), students make their identity development visible. This helps bridge digital literacy with experiential learning pedagogy (Wibowo, 2024).

"How would you represent Indonesian teenagers differently to avoid stereotypes?"

Activity 3

Glocal Voices

Empowering Local Wisdom

Students present local wisdom (e.g., Sago/Papeda culture from Maluku) to a global audience (Manuhutu, 2023). This builds confidence in being an "Intercultural Speaker" (Byram, 2020).

Source Culture EIL Context
Activity 4

Deconstructing the Bias

Critical Inquiry

Analysis of communication breakdowns using the 4-step framework (Noticing, Comparing, Reflecting, Integrating) to move into the "Third Place" (Liddicoat, 2008).

Framework: Byram (1997) & Liddicoat (2008)
