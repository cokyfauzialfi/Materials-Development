Redefining the Teacher's Role

Action Research (AR) is a movement of "reflective practice". It challenges the top-down hierarchy where external experts dictate classroom habits.

"AR is an emancipatory act, allowing practitioners to reclaim professional autonomy by becoming active investigators of their own pedagogical environments."

By merging the roles of educator and researcher, you bridge the divide between theoretical abstractions and the nuanced realities of your specific classroom.

The Systematic Cycle

Phase 1: Plan

Identify a classroom problem and develop an intervention plan with a data-collection strategy.

Phase 2: Act

Implement the change in your classroom environment.

Phase 3: Observe

Systematically collect data on the effects of the intervention.

Phase 4: Reflect

Evaluate results and refine the plan for the next cycle.
