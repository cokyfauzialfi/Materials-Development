    Project Examples

    Reference Only

    Complete worked examples for each stage of the Speaking Materials Development Project, based on "Giving Directions Around School".

    Step 1: Context & Target

    School level/grade: SMP Grade VIII | Proficiency: A1-A2 | Topic: Giving directions around school
    Target Component: Linguistic resources and interaction skills
    Main Challenge: Students need support with English direction phrases and confidence.
    Constraint: Large class, all pairs speak simultaneously to increase STT.
    Decision: Teacher-created maps and role cards.

    Step 2: Pre-Speaking Activity

    Expressions: 1. Excuse me, where is...? 2. It is next to... 3. Go straight. 4. Turn left.
    Check Question: If the library is beside the hall, what can we say?
    Answer: It is next to the hall.
    Model: A: Excuse me, where is the library? B: It is next to the hall.

    Step 3A: Guided Activity

    Type: Information gap.
    Instructions: Work with a partner. Ask and answer questions to complete missing places on your map.
    Note: All pairs speak at the same time for 4 mins. Compare answers with another pair.

    Step 3B: Freer Activity

    Type: Role play (New student & Student helper).
    Prompts: Ask about 3 places. Add one follow-up question.
    Success Criteria: Speak multiple turns, ask/answer 3 places, use 3+ expressions, add 1 follow-up.

    Step 4: Post-Speaking Tool

    Tool: Exit ticket.
    Prompts: 1. Today I could ask about... 2. One useful phrase for me was... 3. Next time I want to improve...
    Revision: Add two follow-up question frames in the next version if students avoid them.
